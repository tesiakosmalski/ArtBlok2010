Historic Research & Development

2010 E Hennepin was originally built in 1927 by a company that was later known as General Mills. After 10 years, 2010 became a campus of 8 buildings.

The front door led to business offices that planned and marketed products like Bisquick and Cheerios.

Labs developed the American Flight Recorder,'The Black Box,'and the Jitterbug torpedo timer.

Authentic artifacts remain of the tools of science.

Artists have made the industrial spaces into exterior platforms for metal working.
